Australia Day Awards 2015

Citizen of the Year, Maureen Bevan

Maureen was nominated for her contribution to the community and many organisations over the years.

Various positions on the Primary School Welfare Club.

High School Parents & Friends Committee.

Always involved with trading tables, raffles and fundraisers with cooking and donations.

Home hairdressing for ladies unable to get out, from the mid-1950s to the early 1970s.

Numerous positions in the Catholic women’s League for more than 30 years.

Knitter of blankets for homeless people.

Burra Bowling Club member and Secretary for a number of years.

Volunteer driver for Community Passenger Car.

Member of Adelaide Women’s and Children’s Hospital Auxiliary from the early 1990s.

Member of the Kooringa and Aberdeen Tennis Clubs from the early 1960s.

Member of Burra Bootscooters 1993-2011.

Volunteer at Burra Uniting Church Op Shop from the early 1990s.

Gathered clothing etc. for Pt Augusta detainees.

Organiser of fundraising dances for Catholic Youth.

Burra Railway Station volunteer.

Manager of Burra Roadhouse for ten years with her husband and also of Paxton Square Cottages for ten years.  [Photo.]
