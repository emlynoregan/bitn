15 November 1989

Burra in the Great War.  Research by Trent Waters, BCS Year 12 History.

In WWI Burra & District sent 700 men, 7 nurses, 4 chaplains and 3 doctors to the front.

Total funds raised were c. £60,000 and £156,000 was raised towards War Loans.

19 June 1914 Burra Military Sports Day raised £29.

9 September 1914 the Burra Town Hall was packed to farewell 20 men.

Letters from soldiers were published regularly in the Burra Record.

10 May 1915 Belgian Flag Day included a procession of decorated motor cars, an auction of gifts and a Belgian Flag, along with speeches, songs etc.  A patriotic concert by The Belgian Bees was held in the Burra Institute.

Burra recruits served in Gallipoli, Egypt, France [and Palestine].

Eleven local men fought at Gallipoli.

In January 1917 there was a display of war souvenirs in Drew & Crewes’ window including a gas mask, salt box, and the nozzle from an anti-aircraft gun.

15 April 1917 the school children gathered at the Rotunda to remember the dead.

By June 1914 four Burra men had won the MC.

The news of the end of the war reached Burra at 9.30 p.m. on 11 November and the following day was observed as a holiday.
