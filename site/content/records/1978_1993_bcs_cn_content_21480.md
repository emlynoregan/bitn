Heritage Week.  Burra National Trust has a display of early washing machines at the State Bank 12-19 October.

Burra Community Day Care Fundraising Committee presents a ‘Musical Extravaganza’ at Burra Town Hall on Friday 12 October featuring Clare Valley Show Band and Burra Ecumenical Choir.  The committee also thanks all involved in making the 60/40 Supper Dance a success.  The profit was about $1,200.

BCS has been undergoing an epidemic of chicken pox; now just about over.

Mrs Joan de Metris is settling into Burra, having previously lived in Africa.

Paul Wetherill & Jane Kappe are newlyweds now living in Burra.  Paul works for Elders and Jane teaches at Clare Primary School.

Birth.  To Alicia & Victor Elliott on 10 September, a son, Jacob Sebastian.

Obituary.  Sylvie, Mrs Howard Griffen of Mt Bryan died on 21 September.

[Born Sylvie Irene Lloyd, 6 November 1923 at Kooringa.  Married Charles Howard Patrick Griffen in 1944.]

Kay Frearson has been successful in getting the position as Education Officer at the Immigration Museum in Adelaide.  She will start there in about a month, after discharging her teaching duties at BCS.

Les & Nan Warnes took their 1913/14 Newton Bennet motor car on the Bay to Birdwood Run on 20 September.

The Burra Show was held in fine weather on Saturday.  Crowds and exhibits were down on those of years ago, but the horse events remain popular.

The Burra Youth Centre will become an incorporated body with the Sports Complex; giving a sound support base and shared public liability insurance.
