BCS Bicentennial Day

On 24 May the Governor Sir Donald Dunstan will officially open the restored Redruth Gaol (First opened in 1856.)  This has been made possible through a Commonwealth [Bank] Grant of $50,000 to the National Trust.

BCS will devote the entire day to celebrating the 200 years of white settlement in Australia.

In the morning 	9-10 a.m.	Historic lessons

10.25 a.m.	Students depart for Redruth Gaol

11 to Noon	Official Opening

12 Noon	Students return to school

12.15 to 1.15 p.m.	Picnic Lunch with parents and community â€“ period costume encouraged

The afternoon will be devoted to competitions, displays and music.

The Police Band and restored horse drawn Black Maria will attend.

3 to 3.20 p.m.	An R-12 Assembly

A Bicentennial Concert is planned for 1 & 2 September.  If you are interested in organising an item contact Anna Stefopolous [Stefanopolous?] at BCS by 27 May.
