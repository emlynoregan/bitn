Offences

Vandals have been damaging the Burra North Playground and tennis courts.  Last week some young offenders were apprehended.

Saddleworth police are investigating a number of cases where vacant houses in the Porter’s Lagoon area have been broken into.

A five-ton stack of sheaved hay owned by L.E. Taylor on the property of E. Landorf, Booborowie was destroyed by fire.

Black Springs School.  The students had an excursion to Adelaide by rail on 4 December.  They visited the Children’s Library, the Public Library, and the top of the AMP Building, witnessed the arrival of the new Governor, Major General Sir James Harrison and visited the Magic Cave, the Botanic Gardens, the Zoo and the SA Parliament.

Mr Bob Farrow was given a farewell after the evening service at the Redruth Methodist Church on Sunday last.  He has been junior circuit steward and preacher’s assistant.  Mr E.T. Baulderstone thanked him on behalf of the town.  Mr Jack Oates sang Where e’er you walk and Elizabeth Oates & Mary Woollacott gave a musical tribute in a specially written song.  A presentation was made of a recording of Handel’s Messiah.

Mr Farrow is moving to become Headmaster of Kadina High School.  He also expressed his support for the Methodist Church’s decision to withdraw from teaching Religious Instruction in State Schools and hoped a more effective means of teaching the subject would soon be evolved.

Burra’s late shopping night will be held on Tuesday, Christmas Eve.  Shops will stay open till 9 p.m.  A merry-go-round will be provided all day Tuesday near the Burra Hotel.  Father Christmas will arrive soon after 9 p.m.  Rural Youth will arrange a dance in the Show Hall to follow the proceedings in the street.
