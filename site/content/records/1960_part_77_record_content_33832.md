Accident/Obituary.  On Easter weekend Peter Wurst of Saddleworth aged 22 was killed in an accident on the Marrabel-Eudunda Road when the car he was driving collided head on with another about five miles from Eudunda.  Peter was a police officer returning to duty in Adelaide, via Eudunda.  He was educated at Saddleworth Primary School and Riverton High School, after which he worked at postal delivery for a number of years before doing farm work with his father.  He was also employed by A.W. Vater & Co. before joining the SA Police Force about two years ago.  He was a prominent player in the Saddleworth-Marrabel United ‘A’ Grade football and played tennis for Saddleworth.  He was a foundation member and instructor of Saddleworth Pony Club and was training for the SA Mounted Police.  He was a leader in the Midland Civil Defence Rescue Parties and active in the EFS.  He was also active in the Saddleworth Show and for several years, even at his young age, was chief steward of the Swine Section.  He was a member of the Saddleworth Buffalo Lodge.

[Peter James Wurst died 25 March 1967 near Hampden, residence Saddleworth aged 22.]
