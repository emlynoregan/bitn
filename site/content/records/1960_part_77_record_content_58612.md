Apex Club

The inaugural meeting at the Burra Hotel on Tuesday last saw 64 Apexians attend.  The Chairman of the Burra Burra DC Mr Roy Jeffery attended and Mr Bob England the Zone 8 President from Adelaide.  The Burra Club at present has 20 members and will receive its charter in about six months, after its contribution to the community and membership is reviewed.  Apex has some 16,000 men in 630 clubs internationally and is for young men 18 to 40.  It was founded in Geelong in 1931 and has spread throughout Australia and SE Asia.  One project in Burra could be the restoration of the old drinking fountain near the caravan park.  Officers for 1971-72: President, Terry Rule; Secretary, Don Topsfield; Service Director, Don Kempster; Treasurer, Murray Mitchell; Programs & Notices, Tony Fillmore; Fund Raising, Allan Day; Social Director, Bob Richter; Membership Director, Russell Campbell; Public Speaking & Debating, Alan Langham & Public Relations, Gordon Wooton.  A meeting on 28 October decided to repair and improve the playground facilities in Burra and to restore the drinking fountain.
