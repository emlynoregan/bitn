BCS 25th Anniversary

About 300 people attended under threatening clouds.

First Principal of the school, Chris McCabe, ceremonially re-opened the school on Saturday afternoon.  Deputy Principal Gus Wenzel was MC and introduced the other principals and first Librarian of the Burra Community Library, Colin Fidock.

Winners of the literary competition; ‘When I Lived in Burra in 1977’:

Open Section	Marie Henson & Rosemary Quinn

Middle School	Bridget Hallion

Junior School	Hayden Noble

Kevin Fahey, who had been principal for ten years when he left last year, mentioned some of the highlights of his time:

1993	Establishment of the Middle School

1995	The town sesquicentenary and back-to-school celebrations

1996	BCS purchased Brewery Hill

1997	Water conservation project on the school oval

1998	Appointment of a chaplain

1999	Funds from leasing Brewery Hill site to Telstra for a phone tower enabled the development of the farm

2001	Erection of the shearing shed

Mike Day, Principal 1987-92, recalled the school’s involvement with the Royal Adelaide Show and its concentration on its agricultural program as highlights, along with the production of ‘The Breaker Morant Retrial’.

[Page 1 carries photos of some former students and the former principals.]

Ron Thamm, Chairman of the School Council in 1978 said that today was a day of thanksgiving and he recalled the role of the Burra Burra District Council in getting the gym built, aided by the buy-a-brick campaign.
