Obituary.  P.H. (Bill) Quirke died at the Royal Adelaide Hospital on Saturday aged 73.  He was the first Member for Burra and a former SA Liberal Minister.  He won the seat of Stanley for Labor in 1941.  After disqualification from Party membership for a year in 1948, Mr Quirke resigned.  Following the redistribution of seats in 1956 he became the first MP for the new seat of Burra, as an Independent.  In 1962 he joined the LCL and was appointed Minister of Lands, Repatriation and Irrigation early in 1963.  He retired from Parliament in 1967.  He was Managing Director of the Clare Co-Operative Winery.  He is survived by a widow, three sons and a daughter.
