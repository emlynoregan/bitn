Burra Conservation Study.  Part III

Section III.  Heading: Open Space and vegetation

Four distinctive types of vegetation contribute to the overall character.

Tall stately River Red Gums (E. camaldulensis) along Burra Creek and the gums of Welsh Place, Paradise and Llwchwr Park Reserve and the Recreation Reserve provide a well treed ‘green’ character to the town, set against bare hills.

The gnarled pepper trees (Schinus molle) which occur throughout the town.
