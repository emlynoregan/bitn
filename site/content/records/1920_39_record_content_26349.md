Obituary.  Mrs E. Cox of Aberdeen died 29 March.  She was born in Hertfordshire 10 October 1850.  The family left for Australia 10 years later in the Coldstream â€“ a voyage of 10 weeks.  Mrs Howse and the children came direct to Kooringa.  Miss Fanny Howse became a dressmaker on leaving school and in 1873 she married Mr Edward Cox, saddler of Redruth, a native of Burra.  Mr Cox died in 1906.  Seven of eight children survive: Herbert Cox (Redruth), Mrs E. Bennetts (Ethel) & Mr Jack Cox (Adelaide), Mrs I. Simpson (Ida of Broken Hill), Mrs S. French (Florence), Mrs B. Attenborough (Gertrude) & Mrs R. Oates (Isabel of Adelaide).  Clement Cox was killed in action in France in the Great War.  There are 19 grandchildren & 5 great-grandchildren.  Mrs George Parks of Pt Pirie is her only surviving sister.  [Born Fanny Howse 10 October 1850.  Two other sons died as infants.]
