Burra EFS-Ambulance Centre was opened by Hon. C.R. Story, Minister of Agriculture on Sunday afternoon 1st June.  [The official party is listed.]  At 12.45 the St John Brigade Band played at the Hospital and at 1.20 it and the service personnel marched from Burra North to the Centre.  Mr E.T. Baulderstone was in charge of the ceremony and welcomed the official party and other guests.  The new centre was erected to give a home to the ambulance and units of the EFS and to the volunteers of both bodies.  Mr T.G. Perry thanked the contractors Messrs D.T. Jacka and Co.  Redruth Sunday School ladies provided refreshments.  G.R. Finch, Chairman of the Burra Burra DC said the ambulance had answered 52 calls in the past year and 17 of them had been trips to Adelaide.  There were four EFS units in the district â€“ Burra, Mt Bryan, Booborowie and Farrell Flat.  Various others spoke.  Rev. J.H. Stephenson blessed the building.  Mr Story congratulated the members of the committee and the contractors on the project and declared the building open.  Mr Moreland on behalf of the EFS and Mr Ron Thamm on behalf of the Ambulance Service accepted the building and expressed their thanks.  After that those present inspected the building before adjourning to the Redruth Sunday School for afternoon tea.
