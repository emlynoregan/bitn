Obituary.  Mrs Bridget Eileen O’Dea, wife of the late John Charles O’Dea died 16 November 1969 aged 71.  She was born at Cavenagh, the second daughter of Mr & Mrs T.F. Kilderry, late of Peterborough and pioneers in the Northern Areas.  Mrs O’Dea was educated at Peterborough Convent School and Convent of Mercy in Angas St, Adelaide.  With her husband and family she was engaged in mixed farming and cattle and horse breeding at Canowie, Hallett.  After her husband’s death she continued with dairying and stud cattle breeding until recently.  She was a devout Catholic and active in the community.  She had a great love of horses, being an adept rider and lover of horse racing.  She is survived by two sons and four daughters: Jim O’Dea (Peterborough), Tom O’Dea (Brisbane), Mrs L. Appleby (Spalding), Mrs E. Young (Croydon), Mrs J. McLean (Maylands), Sister Mary O’Dea (Cemdowie Private Hospital Semaphore).
