‘Burra Boys’ Concert

Publicity Article

The ‘boys’ have been compiling songs about Burra and its history for four years.

It began in 1979 when they won 1st prize in the songwriters’ competition at the State Folk Festival with The Dugout Song.

They then recorded some of their music for Radio 5UV in Adelaide.

In 1980 they got 3rd prize at Mannum’s Folk Festival with Tiddy ’Oggey.

Two of their songs were included in the musical Belle of Burra Burra – The Dugout & Copperhouse Blues.

In 1981 they came close to winning at the Clare Folk Festival with the dramatised song The Ballad of Harold Glee & Stanley Kanoon.

They have performed widely in the district and in Adelaide at the Irish Club and Traitors Gate Folk Club.

They were 3rd and 2nd at the Morgan Folk Festival 1982 with 6 O’clock in the Morning and The Nor-West Bend respectively.

A recording at Studio 202 has been completed.
