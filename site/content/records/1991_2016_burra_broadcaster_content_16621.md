Burra National Trust held its AGM on 29 July.

Maureen Wright reviewed the year:

On 1 July 1997 the Trust took on the lease of the Tourist Office and five tourist sites.

They set up budgets for all sites and a business plan for the branch.

Guides became fully accredited under SATC guide lines.

The Visitor Centre was accredited.

The new manager from 1 July was Jane Cousins.

During the year the treasurer for many year, Joan de Metris, returned to England.

The branch celebrated the 25th anniversary of the re-siting of Peacock’s Chimney with a visit of the Governor of SA, Sir Eric Neal.

Liaison was established with the Schools of Archaeology and Cultural Tourism at Flinders University.

Acting Treasurer Peter Harvey said the branch had a turnover of some $200,000 for the year.

The guest speaker for the evening was Mr Graham Hancock, Asset Manager for the National Trust in SA.

Office bearers for 1998: Chairperson, Maureen Wright; Vice-Chair, Freda Pink; Secretary, Len Newcombe and Treasurer, Avian Pink.
