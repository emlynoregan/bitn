The Burra Show drew a good crowd despite the blustery weather.  Displays of art in the pavilion included Coppertown Ceramics and the new ‘Men Only’ cake competition drew 18 entrants, with the three prizes going to James Barnard, Brandon Hucks and Danny Smith.  Margaret Alderson entered the best exhibit in produce.  Teresa Earle had the best exhibit in cooking and Jean Jamieson the best entry in needlework.  Gillian Barnard was the overall winner in Floral Art, where the best exhibit prize went to Simon Helbig.

Danny Smith submitted the best black and white photograph.  Helen Stockman was the best local photographic entrant and Marlene Phillips had the best colour print of local history.  Jill Foster’s was the outstanding colour print.

In Sheep the Grand champion Ram went to R.H. & H.M. Stockman.

Grand Champion Fleece went to Peter Stockman.

S. & M. Lanyon had the Champion Pigeon and T.W. Broad the Grand Champion Fowl.

Simon Helbig was Prince of the Show, Princess was Kay Carpenter and Junior Princess Caitlin Meyer.

Sarah Spackman won the Teddy Bear prize.  Margaret Alderson had the best exhibit in Fruit & Vegetables.

Damian Quinn & John Shipard won prizes in the ute competition.

Daryl Lee Band played in the vacant shearing shed.
