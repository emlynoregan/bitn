Burra Town Council, 28 October

The Mayor reported on the subject of the union of the Corporation and the District Council.

Burra CWA has donated a further $56 towards a new centre carpet for the Town Hall.

The Tourist Bureau has ruled that the Council should meet the small extra cost of the Caravan Park.

The Hospital Department advises of an increase of $400 or 41% in payment due to the Burra Hospital by the Council: and increase from $973 to $1373.  The Department is to be advised that with the budget adopted and the rate declared by the Council is at a loss to know where they are to get the increase and also drawing attention to the reduction in rates allowed to the hospital for many years.  A copy of this letter will also be sent to C. Allen MP.  If necessary he will be asked to arrange for a deputation on the matter to the Chief Secretary, Mr De Garis.

The Minister of Tourism complains about the public toilets in Burra.  He is to be advised that they are cleaned each morning.

The Department of Agriculture advises a further subsidy of $70 for noxious weed control, for a total of $115.

Burra Small Bore Club lease was renewed for ten years.

Action is to be taken re the safety of the old open tank in the Redruth Gaol.

R.K. Baker was thanked for his work for the short time he has been Town Clerk.
