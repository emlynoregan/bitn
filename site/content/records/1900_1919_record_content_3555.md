Obituary.  Ambrose Harris has died.  He was born 1 Dec. 1826 in the parish of St Nest, Cornwall and arrived in SA in the Sultana on 8 Aug. 1854.  He worked in the Burra Mine and helped to build Schneiderâ€™s Engine House.  In Feb. 1853 he went to the Victorian diggings and remained for 12 months.  He returned to Adelaide and worked as a stonemason, returning to Burra in 1856 and worked again at the mine until 1867 after which he worked on various Government contracts until 1885.  He then joined with Mr James Launder and worked around Burra till 1895, since when he has not been able to do heavy work.  A severe coach accident in 1872 broke his leg and gave him other serious injuries which have affected him ever since.  He resided in Kooringa for upwards of 45 years and celebrated his golden wedding on 24 April 1901.  He leaves a widow and one son, B.G. Harris of Kalgoorlie, and four daughters: Mrs R.H. Steele, Mrs Charles Parks, Miss E.J. Harris of Kooringa and 11 grandchildren.  He was a member of the Burra Lodge MUIOOF since 1859 and also of the Wesleyan Church.   [Died 22 May 1901 aged 74]
