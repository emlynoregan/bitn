Burra Town Council, 6 March

CWA plans for clubrooms in Chapel Street were approved.

Permission for the Aberdeen Tennis Club to use the courts at Aberdeen on Easter Sunday was denied (under the conditions of the lease).

Burra Bowling Club forwarded a cheque for £100 as payment on the old Council Depot.

R.H. Rishbeth offer a trowel used to lay the Town Hall foundation stone in 1874.  Council accepted the offer with thanks.

Local Board of Health

Dr L.G. Miller reported on the inspection of the property owned by Mr Lyle Pearce with the possibility of its becoming a boarding house by Mr F. Dainty.  Dainty’s Roadhouse was also inspected.

Mrs Gosse is to be written to and asked to reply to previous correspondence concerning Paxton Square.

Burra Primary School Welfare Club held its AGM on 7 March.  Elected: President, Mrs D. Field; Vice-Presidents, Mesdames H. Topsfield & M. Mitchell; Secretary, Mrs D. Bannister; Assistant Secretary, Mrs M. Mitcher [sic: perhaps Mitchell] & Treasurer, Mrs Benger.
