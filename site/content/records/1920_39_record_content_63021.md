Obituary.  Mr James Henry Rogers, a prominent farmer of Puni collapsed and died at Hamilton New Zealand on Thursday morning.  He was born at Burra 59 years ago, the fourth son of the late Mr & Mrs John Rogers of Baldina.  After being educated he went to New Zealand 36 years ago.  He was engaged in gold mining at Waihi and at Karangahake before taking a block of land on the Hauraki Plains.  He developed the property and then sold it and 22 years ago acquired a farm at Puni from Mr S. McIlroy.  He was active in local affairs there, being a member of the school committee and Chairman for some time.  He was also associated with New Zealand Co-op. Dairying Co. Ltd and Chairman of the Puni Suppliers Committee.  He died while attending the companyâ€™s annual meeting in Hamilton.  He is survived by a widow, two sons and one daughter: C.J. Rogers of Buckland, C.B. Rogers of Puni and Miss D.E. Rogers of Puni.  There are also six sisters and a brother: W. Rogers (Broken Hill), Mrs W. Pearce & Mrs E. Oates (Kooringa), Mrs A. Pearce, Mrs Aspinall & Mrs W. Watson (Adelaide) and Mrs A. Grose (Broken Hill).
