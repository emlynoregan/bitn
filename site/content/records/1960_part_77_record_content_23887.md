Burra Primary School annual parents meeting was held on 15 March with a pleasing attendance of 40.  The Headmaster Mr I. Auhl gave an interesting address on the Democratic System of Public Schools and reported that the school is to get a new library room.  The school will be 100 years old in 1877.  Elected: Chairman R. Moreland and D.F. Bannister as Secretary-Treasurer.

Circle M Country Style Dancing as viewed on the television program The Country and Western Hour will be provided at Burra Town Hall fortnightly.  Mr & Mrs C. Everett of Brinkworth will be teachers and callers.  All over the age of 14 are welcome.

Burra Swimming Pool.  A wind-up swim for the season was held on Saturday morning with a party of 21 swimmers and guests at the home of Mr & Mrs Ken Williams afterwards.  Trophies were presented to Rosemary Ryan, Michael Broderick, Andrew Ryan, Christine O’Brien, Dawn Britton & Julie Britton.

Burra High School.  Over 100 parents and friends attended the school on 12 March for the induction of the year’s prefects and House Captains.  Headmaster Mr Farrow gave the address.

Prefects are: Brian Simpson (Head), Graham Brook (Deputy), Darrell Field, Garry Disher, Geoffrey Collins, Trevor Willis, Jan Seekamp (Head), Jill Przibilla (Deputy), Vicki Standing, Susan Murphy, Lynette Winders and Naomi Dare.

Night Classes.  A high enrolment has been received for Dressmaking and a second class may have to be established.  A few places remain in typing and woodwork.
