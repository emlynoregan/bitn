Mrs Reta Jennison has been awarded the British Empire Medal in the Queen’s Birthday Honours for dedicated community service over many years.  She has also received a congratulatory telegram from the PM Hon. John G. Gorton.  Mrs Jennison has lived in the Burra area all her life.  In 1947-48 she was Mayoress when her late husband was Mayor.  In 1947 she was appointed a JP.  She was foundation President of the Primary School Parents & Friends’ Association from 1939 for eleven years and was secretary for three years.  She was also a member of the High School Council for a number of years and during WW II was active in the Fighting Forces Comforts Fund and other patriotic committees.  She has been active in the Burra RSL Auxiliary, being its President and Vice-President for a number of years.  She joined the Burra North Red Cross in 1941 and remains a member, having been its President for 23 years as well as being a member of the Burra CWA for twenty years, its President for eight years and Secretary for four.  She has been a Vice-President and member of the Burra Hospital Women’s Auxiliary for many years and a Vice-President and member of the Burra Branch of the Adelaide Children’s Hospital Auxiliary.

She is a member of the Burra Burra Branch of the National Trust and of the Lewis Trust controlling Paxton Square.  She joined the Redruth Methodist Guild in 1926 and has been its secretary for 43 years and is also a member of the House Committee for the Methodist Manse.  She has been choir Mistress at Redruth church and served with the Burra Choral Society when it was active.  A member of the Burra benevolent Society for 27 years and its secretary for ten.  She has served on committees for Freedom from Hunger, the Swimming Pool, Town Hall and Carol Evenings.  We warmly congratulate her on being finally recognised for her services to the community.
