The Nature Conservation Society’s Threatened Species Information Day was hosted at Burra on Sunday 8 May.  Regional Threatened Fauna Ecologist from the Department of Environment and Heritage, Julie Schofield, spoke on the five species that are her focus: Pygmy Bluetongue, Kreftt’s Tiger Snake, the Common Brushtail Possum, the Yellow-footed Rock Wallaby and the Western Whipbird.  Amber Clark, the Threatened Flora Ecologist spoke on threatened plants.  Joe Quarmby spoke on threatened orchids.  Tim Milne spoke on the life of the Pygmy Bluetongue.  There was a field trip in the afternoon.  [Further details are printed.]
