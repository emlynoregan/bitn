That the Exploration Licence, once awarded not be transferable to any other party.

A Conservation Management Plan be prepared prior to exploration, by a suitable expert with recommendations for appropriate cartilage* [sic] to guide, inform and limit exploration and mining activities.  The plan to be adopted only after appropriate community consultation by the Department of Environment and Heritage.

[* The Council used the obscure word ‘curtilage’ – a mediaeval term for an enclosed area around a house etc.]

An Archaeological Investigation of the area to be explored to form fart of the Conservation Management Plan.

A detailed Hydrological Impact Study be prepared and its impacts understood before exploration commencing.

Details of locations of drill holes be provided to Council and approved before exploration commences.

Details of [the impact of ] noise and vibrations on residents, the stability of the open cut, the town and historical structures be provided, understood and approved by Council prior to exploration.

A Visual Impact Statement be provided and approved by Council prior to exploration.

Council provided the submission on behalf of the Goyder community generally and the Burra community in particular as well as in its capacity as the owner of the greater part of the reserve.
