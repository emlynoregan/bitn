Burra Burra DC

Council decided to borrow $200,000 to assist in funding the STEDS common effluent extension.  The loan to be for a ten year term.

Premier Dean Brown will be invited to Burra to discuss the Morgan Road and economic development.

Booborowie Hall stage will get a facelift with Council ordering new curtains at a cost of $1,987 â€“ assisted by a donation from Booborowie CWA.

Council will give $300 to the Skin Cancer Research Foundation to aid in a cancer education program at BSC in 1995-96.

Council agreed to maintain the new command vehicle given to the local CFS.

The Mid North Regional Development Board has appointed Mr David Stacey as an Agricultural Development Officer to work out of the Clare Town Hall.

Burra CWA recently hosted the Warnes Group Conference at the Burra Sports Complex.  Guest speaker was Mrs Carolyn Cooke, granddaughter of CWA founder in SA, Mrs Mary Warnes.  About 100 representatives attended as the Warnes Group celebrated 50 years.  The Burra Branch this year had 15 members and 3 Life Members.
