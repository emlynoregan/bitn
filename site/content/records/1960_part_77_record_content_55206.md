Burra Primary School Parents held their annual meeting on 1 March.  Over 40 parents and teachers attended.

The Welfare Club had spent more than $1,200 in the year.  [The new school committee is listed.]

Outgoing Chairman R.A. Moreland reported:

The oval had been ripped up, top dressed, regularly watered and cut and is now in good condition.

The walkway to Burra North benefits children from there and provides a short cut to Burra Oval.

A wet sports day did not dampen the childrenâ€™s enthusiasm.

Hallett Sports Day was on 24 April.  Burra was again successful.  This year Burra is host school.

The Education Department changed the type of playground equipment that is approved, involving expenditure of c. $400 on new equipment.

The school market garden, in lieu of a cropping program, was successful.  Yesterday morning $100 worth of onions was pulled.

Mr & Mrs Tom Padbury have donated an Honour Board to record the names of winners of the Andrew Tennant Award.

Staff.  In Mat we lost Miss Spratt as Headmistress and Mr Sid Allan became the new Headmaster.

The School Welfare Club has again been active and spent more than $1,200 on the school during the year.

The committee has received a grant of $100 to be spent on maintenance etc. of the school grounds.
