Burra Town Council has accepted recommendations from the Road traffic Board concerning traffic in Market Square.  Senior constable Harvey recommended banning parking on the western side of the Square between the Serv-Wel store and the cycle rack near Gill’s store.  On the south side of the Square from Thames Street to Chapel Street parallel parking only to be allowed.  A parking area for about 20 cars is available in front of the RSL Hall.

The Council will also enforce the By-Law XXVII requiring taxi and hire car licences.

Clare High School teams visited Burra High School on Friday 25 June.

The teams were ‘A’ Football and B, D, & F Basketball for Burra and for Clare ‘B’ Football and A, C, & E Basketball.  Clare won all except the C Basketball game.

Stanley Teachers’ Association met at Burra High School on Monday 21 June.  Mr R.M. Farrow, Headmaster of Burra High School, spoke on ‘Catering for all: has the American High School anything to offer.’  Mr Farrow has been a Fulbright exchange teacher in the US.

Mr & Mrs Barrie Biggs and their family of two young sons will arrive in Burra on 3 July to begin business.  They have purchased Sandy’s Serv-Wel Store and will begin trading on 5 July.  Mr & Mrs Biggs are both country born and bred.  Mr Biggs is a keen photographer and golfer and a football supporter.  Mrs Biggs has been an ‘A’ Grade country district tennis player.
