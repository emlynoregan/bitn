The 1960 Season.  This harvest has seen the best grain intake for years and direct trucking has helped storage problems.  Burra took in:

Premium Grade Wheat	490

Soft Wheat	14,638

Hard Wheat	   8,726

Estimated Total	27,500  	[bags]

The compound at Burra Station is full.

Last season saw only 5,542 bags of wheat and the 1958-59 season 18,000.

Barley this season:

Malting Barley	1095 bags

3rd Grade	2063 bags

4th Grade	3882 bags

Total	7040 bags	(To Saturday – with perhaps 1000 to come.)

Hanson area has had to use direct trucking to handle 50,000 bags carted in the area with 20,000 estimated to come.

Last season’s intake was 12,500 bags and the 1958-59 intake was 33,000 bags.

This year Hanson depot handled 23,000 bags of barley compared with 6,000 last season and 12,500 in 1958-59.

Manoora has handled a record 26,000 bags of barley compared with 18,000 in 1958-59.

Of the 30,000 bags of wheat 4,392 are of inferior quality due to star thistle.

Mt Bryan barley carting has finished with 9,000 bags and wheat to date is 28,000 bags and the estimated 4,000 to come will fill the stack to capacity.

Hallett has so far received 50,000 bags with a considerable amount to come.
