Review of the Year 2005

January

CFS teams aided Eyre Peninsula Fires.

Big fundraiser for Eyre Peninsula Fire Relief Fund raised $21,000

Burra Citizen of the Year was Celine Stockman.

Burra Junior Citizen of the Year was Katie Thomas.

February

AGL announced a proposed 90-135 megawatt wind farm to supplement its Hallett gas fired plant.

Cricketer Jeff Thomson spoke at the Burra Sports Complex.

Rock ’n’ Roll Weekend attracted record crowd.

March

National Fleece Competition held at Burra Merino Field Days.

Scouting re-established at Burra.

Andrew Glen & John Arthur given awards by SA Great.

April

Fuel tanker rolled over on the Burra By-pass.  Damage put at $200,000.

Mt Bryan Uniting Church held its closing service, having opened in 1937.

Removal of asbestos sheeting from BCS roofs announced.

Tony Ryan’s induction into the Shearer’s Hall of Fame was announced.

May

Antique Fair reported good sales.

Redruth Town Crier from Cornwall visited.

Jessie Gurunathan, winner of NZ television’s ‘Miss Popularity 2005’ visited Burra.

Burra hosted the inaugural state-wide Gasshuku – an intensive Karate workshop/contest.

June

Chelsea Evans & Katie Thomas were finalists in the Rostrum Voice of Youth.

BCS livestock escaped a fire in the Agricultural Studies Block.
