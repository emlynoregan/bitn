Obituary.  Keith John (Peter) Hammat died suddenly at Clare Hospital on 13 August after a short illness.  He was born in Burra 12 August 1915.  He attended Magill Boys School and Scotch College.  In 1941 he married Gladys Piggott of Spalding.  He worked in his early life on family properties of Bundaleer and Cutana Stations and then settled on land north of Bundaleer.  He was an active member of Spalding Amateur Swimming Club, timekeeper with the SA Amateur Swimming Association and officiated at many country carnivals.  He was a keen supporter of and a donor to many district projects.  In 1966 he was appointed a JP.  He is survived by a wife and three sons: Glenn, Robert and Phillip and also by his father J. Hammat of Clare and two sisters, Mesdames Bowman & McPherson.
