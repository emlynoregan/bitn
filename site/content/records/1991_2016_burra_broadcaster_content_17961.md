Review of Important Events of 1998

Completion of the sealing of the Burra-Morgan Road – Highway 64 and the Bicycle Blitz to mark the occasion of its opening by Dianna Laidlaw, Minister for Transport and Planning.

Closing of the Eudunda National Australia Bank, leading to enquiries about Credit Unions.

Jan Tiver & Meryl White were Australia Day Citizens of the Year and Heidi Thompson was Young Citizen of the Year.

Creation of WEBLogic – an internet site for Burra, thanks to Dick and Helen Cleland.

Australia Day recognition of the work of Meals on Wheels.

Formation of the new Burra Tourism Association, with Bob Howell as President.

Dawn Broderick won 1998 Burra Women’s Bowls Championship.

Bradtkes of Mangalo Stud, Yongala won Merino Capital Ram Champion Pair.

Governor Sir Eric Neal visited and met SRC members at the school.

Basketball Grand Final was won by Rebels and Phantoms in the women’s competition and by Pacers in the men’s.

Mobile phone towers approved for Hallett and Eudunda, with a third expected for Burra.

Sporting Car Club of SA had a Burra-Morgan trip in May that included Les & Nan Warnes’ 1913 Newton Bennett.

School sports day on 26 March was won by Steele House.

School Swimming Carnival was won by Jennison House.

Tennis Grand Finals: A Grade to Spalding White, A2 Grade to Copperhouse, B Grade to Booborowie and Junior to Kookaburra.

North Eastern Association Ladies’ Bowls Pennant was won by Booborowie for the first time.

5th April Australian Country Games saw competitors come from 31 Little Athletics Clubs. Five medal winners came from Burra: Kali Wall (3), Ric Giles (3), Travis McBride (5), Luke Spackman (5) and Tina Williams (4).

Burra Visitor Centre received a $10,000 grant to aid in promotion.

1998 Sheepdog Trials hosted by Mid North Sheepdog Club at Burra over Easter.

Jowett Car Club visited for Easter weekend.

Roadworks to upgrade Kooringa Road were started.

7th Annual Antiques Fair & Cultural Weekend 2 & 3 May brought 16 dealers and 1,400 people attended – as well as cultural attractions.

Cultural Tourism & Archaeology students from Flinders University spent a week studying the remains of Burra’s dugouts.

Mary Warnes Community Room officially opened by her granddaughter Carolyn Cooke.

Crops this year enjoyed near perfect sowing conditions.

Tourism grants this year included: a one-off grant of $10,000 for local heritage purposes, $3,600 for a landscaping plan for Paxton Square, $877 to BCS to support sporting activities, but Goyder Council’s bid to get $795,000 for a Copper Interpretive Centre was unsuccessful.

School successes included: Class 3-4 won the National Trust’s ‘living health school’ art competition prize of $2,000 worth of computer package, two girls were among the six finalists in the Australia-wide Henry Lawson Society Literary Award, former student Bronwyn Kaiser graduated as dux of her TAFE course in Justice Administration and Heidi Thompson of Year 8 was the state winner of the Feed the World 2020 competition organised by the SA Grain Industry Trust Fund.

Jamestown Drama Group presented Brigadoon at Burra Town Hall and raised $500 for the Burra Hospital Ladies’ Auxiliary.

Rivet ʼn Tuft brought mime, circus skills and storytelling.

Boh Wall was Junior Sportsperson of the Year

Jeannie Kelly was senior Sportsperson of the Year.

Dianne Day was awarded for Service to Sport.

BCS won a computer simulation business competition in an Australian Business Week program in Term 2.

Burra Golf Club obtained a loan of $175,000 to carry out the greening of its course.
