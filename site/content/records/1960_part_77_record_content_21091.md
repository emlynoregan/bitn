River Murray Water for Burra

The Minister of Lands Hon P.H. Quirke has informed us that cabinet has approved the extension of Murray water to Burra and Booborowie.  There will be a pumping station near Hanson with three pumps and total power of 165 BHP.  There will by 44,000 ft of 10 inch AC pumping main along the main road adjacent to the railway line leading to the existing Burra Tanks.  The scheme is based on an annual consumption of 60 million gallons.  The Booborowie scheme will supply the town and some farmlands that have petitioned for supplies, but some farms are too high to be supplied.  There will be 33,600 ft of 8 inch AC feeder main from the Morgan-Whyalla pipeline to the township with 46,800 ft of 6 inch, 4 inch and 3 inch AC reticulation main at an estimated cost of Â£50,000.  Provision is made for an 8 inch spur main eastwards to supply a future service tank.
