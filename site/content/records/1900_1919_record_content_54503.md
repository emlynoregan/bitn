Obituary.  Charles Duell died at his residence at World’s End on 8 March from the effects of the accident sustained on 1 March, without regaining consciousness.  He was born at Beaumont, Adelaide and later took up farming and grazing with his parents at Kapunda, Eudunda and finally at World’s End, where he has resided since 1871.  His death removes one of the World End’s district’s best farmers.  He was an active member of the World End Methodist Church and was church steward.  He is survived by a widow, two sons and three daughters: T.C. Duel, Clem Duell, Messrs R. Baynes (World’s End) & O. Baynes (Booborowie) & Miss M. Duell.  His 2nd son, Jock [John] was k.i.a. in Egypt in 1916.  [He was 66.]
