Miss Gertrude M. Jordan wrote to us recently from Ashfield, Sydney, where she lives in a house called ‘Kooringa’.  Aged 90, she is the last of a family of 10 and she lived in Burra till aged 16.  The family moved to Broken Hill in October 1898.  In January 1908 she went to Sydney and taught at various schools until retiring as an Infant School Headmistress in 1945.  Mr Jordan, her father was a watchmaker and her grandparents lived next door.

[Gertrude May Jordan 1882-1973.  She was a sister to the famous Arthur Jordan 1873-1965, who climbed to the top of the Smelts Chimney when aged about 12.  See an account at 57, 45, 10 Nov. 1936, page 3.]
