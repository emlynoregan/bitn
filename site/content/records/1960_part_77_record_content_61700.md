The book Charles Hawker – Soldier, Pastoralist, Statesman by Mrs K.L. Needham has been given to the Institute Library.  He was born at Bungaree 16 May 1895 and was an outstanding scholar and sportsman.  He received crippling injuries when twice wounded in WWI.  After the war he travelled widely, learning much of agriculture and secondary industry.  He entered Federal Parliament in 1929 as Member for Wakefield and soon became a Cabinet Minister.  In 1932 he was Minister for Commerce.  He was killed in the crash of the aircraft Kyeema on Mt Dandenong in Victoria 25 October 1938.
