Mr Frederick William Pohlner of Bencubbin district of Western Australia died on 30 April aged 66 following a short illness.  He was born at Hallett SA and was one of a family of ten children.  He joined the AIF in 1914 and later transferred to the Australian Flying Corps.  After the war he returned to farming with his father near Peterborough and later took land of his own at Copley in 1922, selling it in 1926 to come to WA.  He was a keen sportsman and played golf and was greatly interested in the affairs of the local football and cricket clubs.  He is survived by a widow and a daughter.

[Born William Fredrich Godfrey Pohlner 29 March 1894 Hallett.]
