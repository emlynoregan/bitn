Weekend School 9-11 May.  The Department of Adult Education at the University of Adelaide will run the school on The Historic Copper Towns of SA.  In their heyday these were the mainstay of the colonial and then the state economy and in the mid 19th century the main suppliers of copper to the world.  The aims of the school are:

To give an account of the fascinating social and industrial history of the copper towns.

To stimulate discussion and action on the care, understanding and preservation of their historic relics.

The school begins with lectures in the University of Adelaide’s Lecture Theatre No.5 Napier Building on Friday evening and Saturday morning and afternoon.  On Sunday there will be a guided tour to Kapunda and Burra.  You can enrol for the lectures only.

Friday 7.45 p.m. Copper SA by Dr John Tregenza, Senior Lecturer, History Department, University of Adelaide.

Saturday 10.30 a.m. The Geological Background to Copper Mining in SA by Mr Keith Johns, Supervising Geologist, Mineral Resources Division SA Department of Mines.

Saturday 2 p.m. Burra in Historical Perspective by Ian Auhl, Burra Branch of the National Trust.

Saturday 4-5 p.m. Panel Discussion: Preserving the Historic Copper Towns.

The excursion will depart the University of Adelaide 8.30 a.m. Sunday 11 May and visit Kapunda Copper Mines before having lunch at Princess Royal Station – members must take packed lunches.  Then a tour of Burra town, mine are, old gaol and museum.  After refreshments at the CWA Rooms the coach will depart for the city at 5 p.m.

Fees: for the lectures and refreshments and buffet luncheon $4 and for the coach excursion $3.50.
