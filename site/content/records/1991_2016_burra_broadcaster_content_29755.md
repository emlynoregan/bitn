Review of 2002

January

Record grain harvest.

5 local fire-fighters went to NSW to assist in fighting bushfires.

Burra Swimming Club’s first inter-club carnival.

The Premier toured the site for the Hallett AGL Power Station.

Sharyn Robert was Australia Day Citizen of the Year.

The restored Sir Hubert Wilkins Homestead was opened.

Tom Padbury received the OAM and Jock Duncan the AM.

February

Burra Silo received a record 11,907 tonnes for the season.

A young male camper on Caroona Station was rescued by helicopter.

Redbanks was gazetted a Conservation Park.

Burra Community Development planned its Youth Leadership Program.

The Rock ’n Roll Festival was a success.

Graham Kellock & Di Day were Bowls Champions.

March

Jazz in the Monster Mine was a sell-out success.

Exhibition at the Gallery of the works of Allyson & Marie Parsons.

The Pascoe Collection of Merino sheep installed at the Town Hall.

Merino Field Days reflected buoyant times.

Terowie celebrated General MacArthur’s 1942 visit & film maker John Paterson McGowan who was born in Terowie in 1880 and started his acting career in 1912, scripting or acting in over 275 films and dying in Hollywood in 1952.

April

Council declined to replace the old red phone box at Burra North despite a petition.

Burra SES moved to new premises in Smelts Road.

A car removed most of the former Courthouse Hotel’s verandah posts.

160 attended Anzac Day Dawn Service.

May

Goyder Council office being renovated.

Adchem made its first shipment of basic cupric carbonate (malachite).

Over 1,400 came to the Antique Fair.

The new National Parks building was opened.

Ian Falkenberg was announced as Ranger of the Year.

Burra Town Hall received a grant to upgrade facilities.

June

The former Hallett Railway Station, now a walkers’ hut, was upgraded.

Former Mt Bryan School playground equipment relocated to Sir Hubert Wilkins Park.

Obstetrics services at Burra Hospital ceased at the end of the month.
