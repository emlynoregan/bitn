CWA.  The Annual Warnes Group Conference was held at Burra Town Hall on Wednesday 26 April.  Group President Mrs W. Klem of Black Springs introduced the President of the Burra Branch Mrs Baulderstone who welcomed all.  Members decided to continue with the Group Objective of training a pre-school teacher for the children at Musgrave Park on the Northwest Aboriginal Reserve.  Mr K. Martin, Research officer for the Department of Aboriginal Affairs, gave an interesting address on the works undertaken by his Department.  Mrs L.J. Burton, Hon. State secretary of the CWA, spoke on branch activities and aspects of CWA work.  As guest speaker Miss Ruth Gibson took UNESCO’s Campaign Against Illiteracy as her topic.  Thirteen tables of handicrafts were staged by branches and winners were auburn and Riverton who both had 39½ points.  Mrs Klem was re-elected President and she named Mrs G. Harding of Black Springs as her Secretary, Miss F. Pattulo of Clare was re-elected Handcraft Secretary.  A trading table convened by the Mt Bryan Group raised $43.42 for the Musgrave Park Fund.
