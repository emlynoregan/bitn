“Come Out ’85” begins in Burra on Sunday 5 May with a balloon release and art display.

On 6 May the Junction Theatre Co. will perform Next year’s Model, a play about apprenticeships and employment in the car industry – aimed at Years 9-12.

On 8 May the Bread & circuses Community Theatre presents Frames, which looks at multicultural Australia and is directed at Years 8-12.

On 9 May Lightning Creek Band presents Instruments Fantastic, live music with more than forty instruments and including stories and mimes for the R-Year 7 group.

There will be a Family Heritage Picnic on Friday 10 May in which participants are encouraged to come in colonial dress.
